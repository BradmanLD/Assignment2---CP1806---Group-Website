The Barrier Reef Orchestra Invests $34,000 in New Harp

The $34,000 instrument is the newest addition to the Barrier Reef Orchestra's repertoire, and Leah Li say it's a privilege each time she gets to play it. She said "It is a beautiful harp, it has a very very beautiful sound". The harp weight 30 kilgorams and has 47 strings. Leah likens the string to the white keys on a piano, while the pedals are the black keys. At home Leah practices on a much older harp, but she says it gets the job done.

"I have [had] an old harp for about 15 years now, the sound is really good but mechanically you just need to tune up ... You actually need to tune the harp mechanically becuase there's so many different pins and so many pedals ... [it's] a lot of work to maintain it." - Leah Li

Orchestra president Andrew Ryder says the harp is a great addition to the ensemble; previously they had to hire a harp and fly a harpist up especially for their performances. Luckily Leah had just moved to Townsville whent he Orchestra purchased the harp.

"It's a wonderful instrument visually but of course the sound of the harp is just fantastic live ... It's immediately identifiable and it adds so much to the Orchestra ... we wish it would come with a truck as well to move it around" - Andrew Ryder

The Barrier Reef Orchestra's first performance from the year is this Sunday, Mrach 29th at the Townsville Civic Theatre, and will feature Leah Li on the harp and flute player Jennifer Bradstreet
