MEMBERSHIP

In 2013 our membership numbers and financial position remained strong.

  • We cover 60% of our expenditure with box office incom
  • We cover the other 40% of our expenditure with grants, sponsorship, and philanthropy
  • Our attendance numbers are growing considerably

For this I would like to thank:

  • Alan Nixon who is retiring after seven years as Secretary
  • Annettee Ellis who has been Treasurer for seven years and continuing
  • Stephen Frewen-Lord as Concert Master
  • The tireless efforts of several rehearsal conductors

SPONSORS

Support continues from our sponsors:

  • Townsville City Council continues as our principal sponsor, which covers our core operational costs
  • The Wheel Family Charitable Gift Fund
  • Sturt Business Centre
  • ASAP Printers
  • Signs of Excellence
  • Australian Concerto and Vocal Competetion

We would also like to welcome our new sponor Wilmar Sugar

FUNDRAISING

A new fundraising approach in 2013 was our Benefactors appeal. This has had a good start with 17 Benefactors currently enrolled and the first renewals are about to commence. This particular avenue of fundraising will stregthen over time and prove to be a very reliable source of income provided it is proactively managed.

2013 saw our first encouragment awards. Two yong orchestra members - Craig Land and Lexie Gorton - each received a well deserved $500 award in recoginition of their enthusiastic participation in the orchestra. The committeeis continuing the encouragment awards into 2014. I would like to thank all the players who together produced such excellent performanced in 2013.

FAREWELL

As I am now retiring as President this is my last report.

Over the last five years it has been gratifying to see the Barrier Reef Orchestra make considerable progress.

In 2009 the orchestra was

  • Drawing an auidence of 150 people or less
  • Had less than $10,000 in reserves
  • Performed as an orchestra of 40 players

The Orchestra today

  • Draws an auidence of 300 to 400 people
  • Has more than $70,000 in reserves
  • Performs as an orchestra of 60 to 70 players

This great success has been made possible by the committee members, sponsors, benefactors, and philanthropists. I am confident this support will continue under Andrew Ryder's presidency and the Barrier Reef Orchestra will retain and enhance its position as one of the leading organizations in Townsville's arts community.
